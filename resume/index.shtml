Short Bio (Updated July 27th, 2011)

    I was born in Guangzhou, P.R. China. Guangzhou is located in the very south of China and is known as the "Southern Door of China". I attended Kindergarten, elemental school, middle school, high school, and college in Guangzhou. I graduated from junior high in 2000 and high school in 2003, all from the Guangzhou No.2 Middle School.

    I attended South China Normal University in 2003, and was initially in an honor program in social sciences (mainly in Economics, Management and Psychology). I then tranferred to Department of Economics after 3 semesters of comprehensive studies in social sciences.My first interests in Economics were Macroeconomics and Development Economics. But after I met my undergraduate supervisor, Dr. Zhiqiang Dong, a game theorist, in my senior year, plus professors in other universities, my interests rapidly changed to Microeconomics and Game theory, as well as Behavioral Economics and Experimental Economics.

    My undergraduate thesis was titled "The Evolution of Cooperation Theory", under Dr. Dong's supervision. It was written in English instead of Chinese because I planned to study abroad and wanted to show my proficiency in English. It got the highest score among all theses in the year I graduated.

    I graduated from the South China Normal University in year 2007. Due to the fact that I took TOEFL and GRE exams late, I did not apply for universities in US in year 2006 so I missed the chance to enroll in fall, 2007. In 2008, I applied for 10 universities including Kansas State University, and decided to attend K-State.

    During the year when I was preparing for study abroad, I read books and papers, besides translating some papers and books for different professors as part of some publication programs. I flied to the States on August 9th, the day after the 2008 Beijing Olympic Ceremony.

    First enrolled as a Master's student, I had different plans in my head when I got here at K-State. But all of the plans at that time link to a fact that I have to find a major advisor and have a paper published. Somehow, now, I'm more than succeed in this goal.

    I decided to stay in Kansas State University for my Ph.D. study after having completed a paper with Dr. Yang-Ming Chang. Now I have completed couple more and keep working. I taught couple sessions of Intermediate Microeconomics (ECON520). I also taught Principles of Microeconomics (ECON120) and Principles of Macroeconomics (ECON110). I always feel that I am excited about teaching.

    I got married with Daru Zhang on May 6th, 2011. I will be on the job market this year and will attend the 2012 AEA/ASSA meetings in Chicago, IL.
